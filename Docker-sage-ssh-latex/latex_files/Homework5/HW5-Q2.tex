    $\textbf{Question2}$

You've only made six trips in your brand new car. The trip distance (x
km) and the amount of fuel used by the car (y liters) are shown in the
following table \\ 

$\begin{matrix}  x km & 14 & 35 & 80 & 116 & 145 & 160 \\ y liters & 2.5 & 6.2 & 10.8 & 13.6 & 15.4 & 19.7 \end{matrix}$ 

What is approximately the correlation between the variables x and y ?

$\textbf{Answer}$

    To find the correlation between the variables x (trip distance) and y
(amount of fuel used), we can use the Pearson correlation coefficient
formula, which is given by:

$ r =\frac{n(\sum xy) - (\sum x)(\sum y)}{\sqrt{[n\sum x^2 - (\sum x)^2][n\sum y^2 - (\sum y)^2]}}$

Where: - $ n $ is the number of data points. - $ \sum xy $ is the
sum of the products of corresponding values of x and y. - $ \sum x $
and $ \sum y $ are the sums of all x and y values, respectively. - $
\sum x^2 $ and $ \sum y^2 $ are the sums of the squares of all
x and y values, respectively.

Given the data:

 \begin{align*}
n &= 6 \\
\sum x &= 14 + 35 + 80 + 116 + 145 + 160 = 550 \\
